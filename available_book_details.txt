1. Harry Potter and the Sorcerer’s Stone
Genre: Fantasy
Summary: It  introduces Harry, an orphaned boy who discovers he is a wizard on his 11th birthday. He attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry, where he makes friends, learns magic, and uncovers the truth about his parents’ deaths and his own destiny.

2. Pride and Prejudice
Genre: Romance
Summary: The novel follows Elizabeth Bennet as she navigates issues of manners, upbringing, and marriage in early 19th-century England. Elizabeth’s initial prejudice against the wealthy and aloof Mr. Darcy gives way to understanding and love as they both grow and change.

3. Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets
Genre: Fantasy
Summary: Harry returns to Hogwarts for his second year, only to find the school plagued by mysterious attacks on students. The legend of the Chamber of Secrets, a hidden chamber created by one of the school’s founders, Salazar Slytherin, becomes a central mystery. Harry, along with his friends Ron and Hermione, discovers that the chamber has been opened and that a monster is attacking students. With the help of a magical diary, Harry uncovers the truth about the chamber and confronts the heir of Slytherin, ultimately saving the school from danger.

4. Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban
Genre: Fantasy
Summary: In his third year at Hogwarts, Harry learns that Sirius Black, a dangerous prisoner who escaped from Azkaban, is believed to be after him. As Harry delves deeper into his past, he discovers that Sirius was his parents’ best friend and is actually innocent of the crimes he was accused of. With the help of his friends and the new Defense Against the Dark Arts teacher, Professor Lupin, Harry uncovers the truth about Sirius and his connection to his family. The book ends with Harry helping Sirius escape from the authorities, setting the stage for future adventures.

5. To Kill a Mockingbird
Genre: Fiction
Summary: Set in the racially charged South of the 1930s, the novel follows young Scout Finch and her brother Jem, whose father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer defending a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. The story explores themes of racial injustice, moral growth, and empathy

6. The Hobbit
Genre: Fantasy
Summary: Bilbo Baggins, a hobbit, is reluctantly drawn into an epic quest to help a group of dwarves reclaim their homeland from the dragon Smaug. Along the way, Bilbo encounters trolls, elves, and the creature Gollum, from whom he acquires a powerful ring.
